The phrase “All rights reserved” is often used together with a copyright notice. Today, this has no legal significance. In the Copyright Act, by default, all rights are reserved. “All rights reserved” are the formalities of copyrights that the copyright owner maintains or possesses for its own use all the rights granted by the copyright law. It originated in the Convention of 1910 in Buenos Aires, Argentina. It no longer has legal effect in any jurisdiction.
